## The idea

Throw darts at a pizza box of with a dart board stuck inside (which touches all 4 walls of the box), the number of darts landing on the dart board over the total number of darts thrown multiplied by 4 will give you an aproximation for pi, with accuracy of estimation increasing with number of darts thrown.

## How this is

The concept behind this cool trick is that the ratio between the two areas of the pizza box and the dart board are special due to how they were contructed. With a square of sides 2, the area of the square would be 4 units^2 and the area of the circle inscribed inside would be 1^2 x π. Therefore the area of the circle over the area of the square would be π/4 and therefore this multiplied by 4 would give you pi. 
Therefore using this idea, you can estimate a value of π by throwing a large number of darts at the board. The probability of landing on the board would be (area of circle)/(area of square), therefore when throwing many darts you would get nearer and nearer to the value of (darts on board)/(total darts thrown) which as previously shown, when multiplied by 4 would give an aproximation for pi.
