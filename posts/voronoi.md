## The idea

Voronoi diagrams are useful tools in which every point in one 'cell' is closer to the point within that 'cell' than any other point in the diagram. Along with the golden ratio, voronoi diagrams are also very prevalent in nature, being seen in giraffe patterns and dragonfly wings. Voronoi diagrams are also used by architects to create more natural biometric facade patterns.

## What it's Used For

Voronoi diagrams are also extremely useful for city planners. If I was a city planner and I wanted to build a new hospital, park, fire station or police office I would want to know where the most optimal location for contruction would be. In order to find this, I could use a voronoi diagram of all the cities hospitals. Using this diagram, I could see where there is the biggest area on the diagram, meaning people within this area will have the longest drive to the hospital when needed. Therefore in placing a hospital there, you would lower the average drive time to a hospital in the city by the most optimal amount. <br>

These methods are also used for other city and infrastructure optimisation like telecoms and was famously used by John Snow in London, Soho to trace cholera outbrakes and locate the cause of the disease, being in a water pump.
